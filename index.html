Core convictions

Vital truths we gladly confess.

This summary captures the core theological emphasis of a historic Reformed Baptist confession.

God's Word is our final authority

We believe the Scriptures of the Old and New Testaments are breathed out by God, trustworthy in all they affirm, and sufficient for faith and life. The Bible must govern what the church believes, teaches, and practices.

God is sovereign in salvation

We believe that sinners are saved by grace alone through faith alone because of Christ alone. Salvation is the work of God from beginning to end, so all glory belongs to Him.

Christ is the only mediator

Jesus Christ is fully God and fully man, the only Saviour of sinners, who lived righteously, died substitutionally, rose bodily, reigns presently, and will come again in glory.

The church is a regenerate people

We believe the local church is to be made up of those who credibly profess faith in Jesus Christ and walk together under His Word in worship, discipline, discipleship, and mutual care.

Believer's baptism matters

We are distinctly Baptist. That means we believe baptism is for those who personally repent and believe the gospel, and that the ordinances belong to Christ's gathered church.

Holiness is the Christian path

Those whom God saves He also sanctifies. The Christian life is one of repentance, obedience, prayer, perseverance, and joyful devotion to Christ in the communion of the saints.
